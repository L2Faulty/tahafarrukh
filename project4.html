AutoCAD Project – V8 Bottom End Assembly

To sharpen my mechanical design skills and get more hands-on practice with CAD, I decided to create a detailed model of an engine piston, connecting rod, and crankshaft using AutoCAD. My passion for cars — especially the engineering behind Formula 1 and other racing series — really motivated me to take on this project.

For the engine specifications, my model was setup with a 90 mm bore, 70 mm stroke, and 8 cylinders, which together give a displacement of around 3.6 liters. While modeling, I focused on how the piston and connecting rod interact with the crankshaft, ensuring the geometry and alignment reflected real-world mechanics.

What stood out to me was how critical these relationships are for engine efficiency and durability, especially in high-performance applications. This project connected my enthusiasm for cars with real mechanical design work, making CAD feel even more practical and directly tied to motorsport engineering.
