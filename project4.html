AutoCAD Project – Piston and Connecting Rod Assembly

To get better at mechanical design and strengthen my CAD modeling skills, I decided to create a model of an engine piston and connecting rod using AutoCAD. What really pushed me toward this project was my passion for cars, especially the world of Formula 1 and racing engineering. I’ve always been fascinated by how engines work at high speeds, and this project helped me explore that from a design perspective. I hope to one day work in the motorsports industry, and this was a small but meaningful step in that direction.

While working on the model, I focused on getting the proportions and geometry right for both the piston head and lower connecting rod, especially paying attention to how they align. Even though this was a simple project, it gave me a better understanding of how important these components are in a high-performance engine — especially when precision and durability are critical.

To clearly show the overall shape and structure, I’ve attached views that help visualize the model from different angles. This project helped me connect my interest in cars with real mechanical design work and made CAD feel much more practical and relevant.
